Accessories

Set of balls


A pool table ball set typically consists of 16 balls, including one white cue ball, seven solid-colored balls numbered 1 through 7, seven striped balls numbered 9 through 15, and one black 8-ball, used in various billiards games.

Pool Cloth


The pool cloth, also known as the felt, is a smooth, tightly woven fabric that covers the surface of a pool table, ensuring consistent ball roll and play, and is usually made from a blend of wool and nylon for durability and performance.

Pool cue


A pool cue is a long, tapered stick used to strike the cue ball in a game of pool, typically made of wood or fiberglass, and designed with a leather-tipped end for precise control and accuracy during play.

Chalk set

A chalk set for a pool table consists of small, cube-shaped blocks of chalk that players apply to the tip of the pool cue to increase friction and control, reducing the chance of miscuing when striking the cue ball.

Pocket set


A pocket set on a pool table consists of six pockets, typically made from leather or rubber, positioned at each corner and midpoint of the tableâ€™s long sides, designed to capture and hold the balls as they are pocketed during play.

Triangle


The triangle, also known as a rack, is a triangular frame used on a pool table to arrange the 15 object balls in a tight formation at the beginning of a game, typically with the 8-ball centered in the third row.

Spider head


The spider serves as an elevated version of the rest. It becomes useful when the cue ball lies just beyond another ball, making a standard or cross rest unsuitable since the cue must pass over the obstructing ball. In such situations, the spider is the only tool that allows the shot to be played.

Wrest Head / X-Head


The X-head is a metal bracket used in pool tables to securely fasten the table's legs to the frame. Its "X" shape provides strong structural support, helping maintain the table's stability and balance during play.
